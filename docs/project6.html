Analyzing Privacy Policies of Menstrual Cycle Tracking Apps Using LLMs

LLMs Privacy NLP Research

For my thesis, advised by Brian Kernighan, I explored privacy policies of menstrual cycle tracking apps utilizing LLMs. This topic aligns with my work on Astrid Athletics as the protection of health data such as menstrual cycle symptom data is of the utmost importance, especially after the overturning of Roe v. Wade. Data recorded by these menstrual cycle tracking apps can be used against women in court as it is possible to detect pregnancy and abortions through the data collected, potentially leading to prosecution.

I believe that women should have the right to privacy and protection of this data, but oftentimes people do not analyze the privacy policies of apps they utilize, leading to unwanted exposure of data. Through utilizing LLMs to automate the evaluation of the privacy policies of existing menstrual cycle tracking apps, I developed a system to rate the apps in terms of data security and privacy as well as policy language complexity.

Through my work I also explored how to utilize LLMs to produce a consistent and reliable analysis of these privacy policy documents. Once I did so, I was able to determine which existing mobile apps have the strongest privacy policy practices and also whether these policies have language complexity levels that are comprehensible for the general public.

By the end of my analysis using LLMs I had an understanding of the best practices to protect this data and implement it within the mobile app I am developing for Astrid Athletics for optimal privacy levels.

On a privacy policy strength scale from 0-10 with 10 being perfect privacy, the average privacy policy strength score across the top 100 menstrual cycle tracking apps on the App Store was 4.9/10, which exposed the weaknesses of currently existing privacy practices.
